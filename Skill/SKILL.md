# Agreus Family Office Compensation Benchmark 2025

This skill provides comprehensive knowledge from the 2025 Global Family Office Compensation Benchmark Report, a joint publication by Agreus Group and KPMG Private Enterprise based on 585 survey responses and 20 qualitative interviews with family office professionals worldwide.

## Quick Reference

### Key Global Findings
- 62% of professionals received a salary uplift in 2024/25 (40% due to inflation)
- 28% receive a Long-Term Incentive Plan (LTIP)
- Most common LTIP types: carried interest (45%), 3/5-year rolling target bonus (43%), co-investing opportunities (41%)
- 60% receive discretionary bonus; 12% receive formulaic bonus
- 51% have a succession plan
- Cost of running a family office: most commonly 0.6-1% of AUM
- 44% of family offices have two or more locations

### AUM Distribution
- Below $250M: 6%
- $251M-$500M: 21%
- $501M-$1BN: 28%
- $1.1BN-$2BN: 16%
- $2.1BN-$5BN: 8%
- $5.1BN+: 9%

### Team Sizes
- 5 or fewer employees: most common
- 19% have 20+ employees
- 79% of family offices are standalone entities (not embedded in operating business)

## Data Notes

- Survey conducted 2024-2025, published September 2025
- 585 survey respondents + 20 qualitative interviews
- Geographic spread: Europe 52%, Americas 13%, Asia 10%, Australia 10%, Middle East 10%, RoW 5%
- All compensation figures should be interpreted as ranges; individual circumstances vary significantly
