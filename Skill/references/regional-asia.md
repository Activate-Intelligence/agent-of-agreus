# Asia Family Office Compensation

## CEO Profile
- Most common age: 45-54
- Background: Banking, Investment Management, Another Family Office
- 100% male (highest in any region)
- Education: Masters and University degrees common

## Key Markets
- Singapore: Leading hub
- Hong Kong (SAR), China: Traditional wealth centre
- Mainland China: Growing domestic wealth
- Japan: Emerging market
- Other SE Asia: Thailand, Indonesia, Malaysia, Philippines

## CEO Compensation by AUM

Higher AUM correlates with higher compensation, but regional variations significant:
- Singapore: Premium market rates
- Hong Kong: Competitive with global hubs
- Mainland China: Lower base, higher variable potential

## Bonus Structure
- Performance bonus prevalent
- Discretionary more common than formulaic
- Bonus percentages: Typically 20-50% of salary
- Higher potential for investment roles

## AUM Distribution (Asia)
- Below $250M: 15%
- $251M-$500M: 20%
- $501M-$1BN: 25%
- $1.1BN-$2BN: 20%
- $2.1BN-$5BN: 12%
- $5.1BN+: 8%

## Running Costs
- Varies by jurisdiction
- Singapore: Higher operating costs
- Hong Kong: Premium real estate costs

## Family Office Characteristics
- Newer establishments: Many formed in last 10 years
- Multi-generational transition underway
- Growing professionalisation
- Increasing separation from operating businesses

## Gender Distribution
- 100% of CEOs are male (survey finding)
- Overall workforce: Improving female participation

## Working Arrangements
- Office-based culture stronger than Western markets
- Remote work less prevalent
- Relationship-driven business culture

## Key Markets Analysis

### Singapore
- Government incentives (Section 13O, 13U schemes)
- Tax-efficient jurisdiction
- Strong regulatory framework
- Premium talent market
- Growing as Asia-Pacific hub

### Hong Kong (SAR)
- Traditional wealth management centre
- Access to mainland China
- Established professional services ecosystem
- Competitive compensation packages

### Mainland China
- Rapidly growing domestic wealth
- First-generation wealth creation
- Governance structures developing
- Compensation catching up to regional standards

## Talent Dynamics
- Competition for talent from banks, PE, sovereign wealth funds
- Premium for bilingual/multilingual skills
- Cultural fit important
- Loyalty valued

## Key Commentary

> "Families are getting bigger, say, north of a billion dollars, some of them have set up dedicated satellite offices offshore and it's primarily in the US. By doing so, they have access to a dedicated deal flow pipeline." - Former CIO, Consultant

## Regional Trends
- Migration of wealth from mainland China
- Singapore emerging as preferred jurisdiction
- Increasing sophistication of investment strategies
- Growing demand for succession planning expertise
- Technology adoption accelerating
