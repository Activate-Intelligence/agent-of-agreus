# Australia Family Office Compensation

## CEO Profile
- Most common age: 45-54
- Background: Banking, Investment Management, Accountancy
- 79% male (improved from 2023)
- 21% female CEOs (up from 8% in 2023)
- Education: Masters and University degrees common

## CEO Compensation by AUM
- Below $250M: Lower range
- $501M-$1BN: Mid-range
- $1BN+: Premium compensation

## Bonus Structure
- Performance bonus common
- Mix of discretionary and formulaic
- Typical range: 20-50% of salary

### Bonus Drivers
- Personal performance: 36%
- Inflation: 39%
- Business performance: 11%
- Market data: 11%
- Cost of living: 3%

## LTIP
- Types include carried interest, co-investing, rolling target bonuses
- Less prevalent than USA but growing

## Salary Uplift (2024/25)
- Majority received an uplift
- Drivers: Inflation (39%), Personal performance (36%)
- Most common uplift: Less than 5%

## Working Arrangements
- 12% allowed to work remotely
- Lower remote work than global average
- 80% believe they perform a hybrid role

## AUM Distribution (Australia)
- Below $250M: Higher proportion than global
- $251M-$500M: Growing segment
- $501M-$1BN: Significant
- $1BN+: Smaller proportion

## Running Costs
- Varies by AUM and complexity

## Family Office Characteristics
- 60% in operation for 10+ years (mature sector)
- Growing number of establishments
- Lean teams common
- Hybrid roles prevalent

## Gender Distribution
- 31% female (up from 12% in 2023)
- 69% male
- Significant improvement in female participation

## Generations of Wealth Managed
- First/second generation: Most common
- Multi-generational: Growing
- Succession planning increasing focus

## Purpose of Family Office
- Wealth preservation: Primary
- Risk-adjusted growth: High focus (contrasts with global conservative trend)
- Philanthropic initiative: 46% (80% increase from 2023)
- Administration: Standard

## Key Commentary

## Regional Trends
- Emerging as key centre of Family Office growth globally
- Strong legal systems (Federal and State)
- Economic stability
- Growing pool of private wealth
- Migration of wealth from Southeast Asia (mainland China, Hong Kong)
- Sub-continental wealth holder immigration
- Complex tax system but no deliberate incentives
- Geographic ties to Asia markets
- Technology integration accelerating
- Focus on cost management and consolidation
