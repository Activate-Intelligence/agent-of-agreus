# UK Family Office Compensation

## CEO Profile
- Most common age: 45-49
- Background: Accountancy (30%), Banking (30%), Another Family Office (15%)
- Education: Masters (common), University degree
- 85% male
- Most common salary: £198,001-£264,000

## CEO Compensation by AUM

**£198,001-£264,000**: Common across $501M-$2BN AUM
**£264,001-£330,000**: More common at $1.1BN+ AUM
**£500,000+**: Rare, typically $5BN+ AUM

## Bonus Structure

- 85% receive a performance bonus
- 90% discretionary, 22% formulaic
- Most common bonus: Less than 20% of salary

### Bonus Drivers
- Overall fund performance: 40%
- Personal performance: 35%
- Business performance: 15%
- Principal relationship: 5%

## LTIP
- 20% receive LTIP
- Types: 3/5-year rolling bonus, carried interest, co-investing opportunities

## Benefits
- Pension: 70%
- Private healthcare: 69%
- Life insurance: 47%
- Dental: 28%
- Maternity leave: 75%
- Paternity leave: 59%
- Annual leave: 25-30 days most common

## Salary Uplift (2024/25)
- 69% received an uplift
- Most common: Less than 5% (46%)
- Drivers: Inflation (51%), Personal performance (24%), Business performance (13%)

## Working Arrangements
- 57% allowed to work from home
- 12% work remotely full time
- 65% believe they perform a hybrid role

## Location
- 86% based in London
- Other: Guernsey, Isle of Man, Midlands, Cotswolds, Yorkshire, Cambridge
- 35% have second location (most common: Europe, Asia, Middle East)

## AUM Distribution (UK)
- Below $250M: 14%
- $251M-$500M: 14%
- $501M-$1BN: 30%
- $1.1BN-$2BN: 15%
- $2.1BN-$5BN: 13%
- $5.1BN+: 14%

## Running Costs
- Most common: 0.6-1% of AUM (37%)

## Key Commentary

> "Although there have been significant UK tax changes over recent years, the UK remains an attractive location for Family Offices with London as a nexus for many international families." - Nick Pheasey, KPMG

> "The UK will continue to be attractive in the mid-long term as the legal system is well-established, stable, and transparent. Additionally, there is great depth in the Family Office talent pool." - Andrew Layton-Hoang, CFO/COO, Private UK Single Family Office
