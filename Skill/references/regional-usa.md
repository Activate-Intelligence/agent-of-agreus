# USA Family Office Compensation

## CEO Profile
- Most common age: 50-54
- Background: Another Family Office (33%), Investment Management (13%), Accountancy (8%), Banking (4%), Legal (8%)
- Education: Masters (common), University, some Doctorates
- Most common salary: $396,001-$500,000

## Bonus Structure
- 64% receive discretionary bonus
- Less common: formulaic bonus
- Wide range: from less than 10% to 200%+ of salary

### Bonus by Role
- CEO: Commonly 21-50% of salary; can reach 150%+
- CIO: Higher bonus potential, often 51-100%+
- CFO: Typically 21-40%
- Investment professionals: Variable, performance-linked

## LTIP
- Common types: 3/5-year rolling target bonus, carried interest, co-investing opportunities
- Stock options more prevalent than in other regions
- Loans (with/without recourse) used in some structures

## Salary Review
- 87% reviewed annually
- 13% reviewed periodically

## Benefits
- Healthcare: Standard
- Retirement plans: 401(k) common
- Equity participation: More common than other regions

## UBO Involvement

## Succession Planning
- 61% do NOT have a succession plan

## Team Characteristics
- 62% male
- Generations managed: 1 (26%), 2 (35%), 3 (31%), 4+ (8%)
- Team growth: Muted; most teams stable

## Key Commentary

> "The US equity markets have realized a significant recovery since 2022. Many SFOs utilize incentive compensation plans for their key employees. The recovery has likely resulted in significantly increased incentive compensation bonuses." - Tracey Spivey, KPMG

## Regional Notes
- New York and Texas dominate as family office hubs
- Florida growing as tax-efficient destination
- California: Tech wealth concentration
- Higher use of performance-based compensation than other regions
- Greater proportion of CEOs from family office backgrounds
